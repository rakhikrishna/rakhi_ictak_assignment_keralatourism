TRIVANDRUM
"The City of Lord Ananta"

The capital city of Kerala, Thiruvananthapuram or Trivandrum is an appealing blend of a strongly rooted heritage and a nostalgic colonial legacy. Built upon seven hills, this city has long since left the days when it was only used by seafaring explorers behind - today, Trivandrum is a swanky metropolis with quaint urban charm and plenty of scenic places to visit.

PATHANAMTHITTA
"Pilgrim Capital of Kerala"

Pathanamthitta, in southern Kerala, is a town immersed in spirituality and mysticism. Blessed with several ancient temples, it is renowned as headquarters of pilgrimage in Kerala as Sabarimala is situated in the Pathanamthitta district. It is a land of untouched beauty with pristine rivers, lofty mountain ranges and sprawling coconut groves.

ALAPPUZHA
"Backwaters, Beaches and Lagoons - Venice of the East"

Officially called Alappuzha, Alleppey is a city in the South Indian state of Kerala. Bordering the Laccadive Sea, it is known for its wide network of interlinking, palm-fringed canals called backwaters and rejuvenating Ayurvedic resorts. Allepey is also popular for its Houseboat cruises that pass through the serene backwaters, where you can catch glimpses of green paddy fields, choir making activities, beautiful avifauna and witness the life of locals in Kerala.

KOTTAYAM
"Akshara Nagari (City of Letters) of Kerala "

An ancient town located on a hilltop bordered by the western ghats on the east and the thrilling Vembanad Lake on the west, Kottayam is a culturally rich and naturally enchanting destination. Apart from nature's bounty, the town has many laurels to its name. It is a proud town to achieve 100% literacy in India for the first time. Also being the land of several publishing houses and print media, Kottayam is also tagged as the 'Akshara Nagari' meaning 'City of letters'.

ERNAKULAM
"Heart of Kerala"

Ernakulam is the central district in Kerala and is home to its financial and industrial capital, Kochi. The district is considered as the economic and political hub of Kerala. The Cochin Stock Exchange, Kerala Municipal Corporation and Kerala High Court also fall within this district. While it is the most industrial area in Kerala, it also boasts of wonderful tourist attractions that connect man to nature and God.
